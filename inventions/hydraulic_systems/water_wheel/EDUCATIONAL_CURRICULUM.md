## üé® Curriculum Structure

### Module 1: Historical Context & Renaissance Innovation (Week 1)

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand Leonardo's role as hydraulic engineer
- Analyze Renaissance engineering methodology
- Compare historical vs. modern approaches
- Appreciate interdisciplinary nature of Renaissance innovation

#### Content Delivery
```
Lecture 1: "Leonardo the Hydraulic Engineer"
- Milan's canal network and hydraulic challenges
- Leonardo's appointment as "Ducal Engineer"
- Analysis of Codex Atlanticus hydraulic studies
- Renaissance engineering methodology

Interactive Activity: "Virtual Codex Exploration"
- Navigate digital reproductions of Leonardo's hydraulic drawings
- Decode mirror writing and technical annotations
- Identify engineering principles in artistic sketches

Practical Exercise: "Renaissance Design Challenge"
- Design a simple water management system using only Renaissance tools
- Present solutions using period-appropriate technical drawings
- Compare with Leonardo's actual designs
```

#### Assessment Methods
- Historical analysis essay (1500 words)
- Technical drawing interpretation exercise
- Group presentation on Renaissance engineering principles
- Peer review of design challenge solutions

### Module 2: Physics & Mathematics of Water Power (Week 2)

#### Learning Objectives
- Master fundamental hydraulic principles
- Apply mathematical models to water systems
- Calculate power, efficiency, and performance
- Understand energy conversion mechanisms

#### Content Delivery
```
Lecture 2: "The Mathematics of Moving Water"
- Bernoulli's equation and energy conservation
- Continuity equation and mass conservation
- Torque, power, and rotational dynamics
- Efficiency calculations and optimization

Laboratory Session: "Hydraulic Performance Testing"
- Measure actual performance vs. theoretical calculations
- Use flow meters, pressure sensors, and dynamometers
- Analyze efficiency losses and improvement opportunities
- Document findings with scientific methodology

Calculation Workshop: "Engineering Mathematics"
- Solve complex hydraulic engineering problems
- Optimize design parameters for maximum efficiency
- Compare historical vs. modern calculation methods
- Use both manual and computational approaches
```

#### Assessment Methods
- Problem-solving examination (complex calculations)
- Laboratory report with data analysis
- Mathematical modeling project
- Peer review of calculation accuracy

### Module 3: Design & Manufacturing (Weeks 3-4)

#### Learning Objectives
- Master 3D modeling and CAD design principles
- Apply modern manufacturing techniques
- Understand material selection and properties
- Develop precision manufacturing skills

#### Content Delivery
```
Workshop 1: "Digital Renaissance - CAD Modeling"
- Learn professional CAD software (Fusion 360/SolidWorks)
- Create parametric models based on Leonardo's drawings
- Apply modern design principles to historical concepts
- Generate manufacturing drawings and specifications

Workshop 2: "3D Printing the Renaissance"
- Master 3D printing techniques and settings
- Optimize designs for additive manufacturing
- Understand material properties and selection
- Apply post-processing techniques for professional finish

Manufacturing Lab: "From CAD to Reality"
- Print all components using provided specifications
- Apply quality control and dimensional verification
- Assemble components using provided instructions
- Test assembled system for proper operation
```

#### Assessment Methods
- CAD modeling project (complete component library)
- Manufacturing quality report
- Assembly accuracy evaluation
- Final product testing and validation

### Module 4: Assembly & Testing (Week 5)

#### Learning Objectives
- Master precision assembly techniques
- Apply quality control procedures
- Conduct comprehensive performance testing
- Document results with scientific rigor

#### Content Delivery
```
Assembly Workshop: "Precision Renaissance Assembly"
- Learn proper assembly techniques and tools
- Apply torque specifications and safety procedures
- Use precision measuring instruments
- Follow systematic assembly sequence

Testing Laboratory: "Performance Validation"
- Conduct comprehensive performance testing
- Use professional measurement equipment
- Compare actual vs. theoretical performance
- Analyze efficiency and optimization opportunities

Documentation Workshop: "Scientific Reporting"
- Write comprehensive technical reports
- Create professional documentation
- Present findings to peer audience
- Archive results for future reference
```

#### Assessment Methods
- Assembly accuracy evaluation
- Performance testing report
- Scientific presentation
- Peer review of documentation

### Module 5: Optimization & Innovation (Week 6)

#### Learning Objectives
- Analyze system performance for improvement opportunities
- Apply modern optimization techniques
- Develop innovative solutions
- Compare historical vs. modern approaches

#### Content Delivery
```
Optimization Workshop: "Performance Enhancement"
- Analyze efficiency losses and improvement opportunities
- Apply modern engineering optimization techniques
- Use computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulation
- Implement design improvements and test results

Innovation Challenge: "Beyond Leonardo"
- Develop innovative improvements to basic design
- Apply modern materials and technologies
- Consider environmental and sustainability factors
- Present innovative solutions to peer audience

Research Project: "Historical vs. Modern Comparison"
- Compare Leonardo's design with modern equivalents
- Analyze efficiency improvements over 500 years
- Consider cultural and technological influences
- Present comprehensive comparative analysis
```

#### Assessment Methods
- Optimization project with before/after comparison
- Innovation challenge presentation
- Research paper (3000 words minimum)
- Peer review of improvement proposals
